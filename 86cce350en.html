2023 Summary

发表于 2024-01-01 更新于 2024-10-31 阅读次数： 阅读次数： Valine：
本文字数： 3.1k 阅读时长 ≈ 3 分钟

In the blink of an eye, 2023 has ended, and 2024 has already begun. Tomorrow is the first working day of 2024. After much thought, I feel it’s necessary to summarize my year.
After working for several years, it seems like I’ve just been drifting along without ever doing a summary. After a long thought, I decided to write a reflection on the past.

In 2023, I realized that I actually gained quite a lot. Let me discuss it briefly from two aspects: work and life.

Work

Unbeknownst to me, I have been working in Shenzhen for over a year since last September.

Work Experience

Last year, in 2023, I was fired from a company for the first time. Honestly, I was a bit discouraged because the company had asked me to leave. I had never experienced this before, so I thought since the company suggested it, I might as well leave. That evening, while having dinner with a former colleague who was surprised I agreed so quickly without discussing compensation. I was shocked; I didn’t know termination could involve compensation. After doing some research online, I immediately discussed it with my leader upon returning to the office. Initially, my leader did not agree, so I didn’t resign and kept going to work as normal while applying for new jobs. Eventually, we agreed on a compensation.

Skills Acquired at Work

I feel I’ve gained a lot in this area. Previously, I only worked on Lua development and iOS publishing. Early last year, I touched on Google publishing, which was my first encounter with Android. In April, I joined a new company and started using Cocos Creator. The product was almost developed from scratch by me (I was responsible for the entire frontend with a framework taken from someone else), so the pressure was always high, and I was continuously learning. Previously, I only worked on mobile platforms, but this year I also worked on web platforms, which I found quite different. In December, I also worked on Android, which I had never studied before, so I often worked past 10 PM. January was even more stressful because the project was scheduled to launch. Although I had experience in pure business coding, dealing with Android’s native elements (a completely new environment for me) meant I had a lot to learn, and the pressure was immense.

Other

  1. I started a TikTok account, deleted it in February, and then opened a new one. Initially, I thought I could use TikTok to make some side income. However, as of now, I only have over 2000 followers and haven’t found a good way to monetize it, but I’ll keep at it, and put the monetization plans on hold for now.
  2. I started a WeChat public account, did not keep it up, and ended up deleting it by the end of the year.

Summary

Overall, 2023 was a pretty good year, probably the year I’ve worked hardest since graduating.

Life

There have been several significant changes in my life.

Exercise

Since joining the new company, I started exercising regularly (perhaps because I’m getting older), almost every week, including basketball, swimming, hiking, and trekking, sometimes with colleagues, sometimes alone.

Relationships

This is quite a happy development; I found a girlfriend this year, haha.

Plans for 2024

Work

After a year of work in 2023, I’ve realized I still have many deficiencies and plan to learn more in 2024 to address these gaps.

Reading

No matter the level of expertise, I must continue reading, aiming for no less than 12 books.

Exercise

I plan to continue exercising weekly, hoping to improve my freestyle swimming (currently, I only know breaststroke and would like to take classes). I aim to swim a total of 50 kilometers.

Other

I plan to buy a car outright, costing no less than 200,000.
